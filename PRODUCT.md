# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individuals managing personal finances — tracking accounts, importing bank/credit card statements, categorizing transactions, and understanding cashflow. They use it daily or weekly, typically on desktop during focused financial review sessions. They want clarity, not complexity.

## Product Purpose

Fintivi is a calm daily finance home. It exists to make personal financial management feel safe, clear, and frictionless — taking the anxiety out of knowing where your money goes. Success is a user who opens it regularly because it feels helpful, not because they're forced to.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Trustworthy. Precise. Warm. Approachable. Helpful. Modern. Minimal. The voice of a competent friend who explains things clearly without patronizing.

## Anti-references

- Dark navy/gold fintech dashboards — the saturated finance default, avoided.
- Corporate sterile bank UI — cold, institutional, overwhelming.
- Generic SaaS cream — the beige-card-grid AI default.
- Feature-dumped screens — everything visible at once, no hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm clarity** — Finance is stressful. Every screen should reduce cognitive load. One primary action per view. Clear hierarchy. No competing calls-to-action.

2. **Trust through precision** — Numbers must be unambiguous. Monetary values are formatted consistently. Actions have clear outcomes. State transitions (upload pending → preview → confirmed) are visible.

3. **Warm competence** — Approachable without being casual. Professional without being cold. The warmth comes from spacing, typography, and micro-copy — not from decorative color.

4. **Progressive disclosure** — Show what's needed for the current task. Hide complexity until the user asks for it. Upload flow is a guided sequence, not a form to fill.

5. **Best in class** — Premium through restraint. Whitespace, thoughtful alignment, consistent rhythm. Every element earns its place. Remove anything that doesn't serve the user's task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Focus states on all interactive elements. Semantic HTML (nav, main, section, table, button). Reduced motion respected. Color is never the sole carrier of information.
